🌶️ Mexican Culture & Slang Tip

In Mexico, when ordering food, it is exceptionally common and polite to use "Me da..." (Give me...) or "Me regala..." (Gift me...) instead of the literal "Yo quiero" (I want). Using "por favor" adds the perfect respectful touch!

Mexicano Phrasebook

Handy, polite expressions commonly used in Mexican everyday transactions. Click to hear them spoken!

Taquería "Disculpe, ¿me da tres tacos al pastor, por favor?" "Excuse me, could I have three tacos al pastor, please?"
Eating Out "Para llevar, por favor." "To go, please." (Crucial Mexican phrase instead of 'para llevar afuera').
Supermercado "Disculpe la molestia, ¿dónde puedo encontrar...?" "Excuse the bother, where could I find...?" (Highly polite introductory phrase).
Dog Walking "Se llama Pancho y es súper amigable." "His name is Pancho and he is super friendly." (Perfect dog-walk vocabulary).
Dog Walking "Es hembra, tiene tres años de edad." "She is a female, she is three years old." (Using 'hembra' for animal gender).
Shopping "Disculpe, ¿las tiene en número siete, por favor?" "Excuse me, do you have them in size seven, please?" (Using 'número' for shoe size).
